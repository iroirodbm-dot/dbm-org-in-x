About Us

Our History and Vision

Deaf Biblical Ministry (DBM), a registered society under the Society Registration Act, 1860, is a National Award-winning NGO recognized for its pioneering work and profound impact on the welfare of the Deaf in Nagaland and beyond. Established in 1987, DBM is the first and remains the only organization of its kind in Nagaland, dedicated to running a School for the Deaf in Dimapur.

With a vision to provide quality education, equip the Deaf with essential skills for independent living, and serve as a beacon of empowerment, DBM continues to transform lives and uplift the Deaf community. As of 2026, DBM is located in Naharbari, Dimapur (Lane 3, Ward 10), and provides free education and accommodation to over 70 Deaf children annually, alongside serving more than 200 Deaf Church members.

Recent updates include admissions open for the 2026 academic year, all Class 10 students passing in 2025, and ongoing spiritual initiatives, such as welcoming new members to the community in early 2026.

Founders and Team

DBM was founded by Rev. Yanger Walling, who has received numerous accolades for his dedication, including the National Award in 1999, Karmayogi Award in 2008, and the Kevichusa Citizenship Award in 2023. The team includes dedicated staff who have been recognized with awards like the Rotary Award in 2001.

Today, DBM employs Deaf individuals as teachers (5 as of 2024, with continued growth), and collaborates with linguists, educators, and government bodies to advance Deaf rights and education.

Our Timeline

  • 1987: DBM established as the first School for the Deaf in Nagaland.
  • 1999: Rev. Yanger Walling receives National Award.
  • 2004: National Award for Hearing Impairment.
  • 2008: Karmayogi Award for Rev. Yanger Walling.
  • 2009: National Award as Employer of Persons with Disabilities; Management of EMRS begins (until 2024).
  • 2022: Temsutola Jamir (alumni) receives President's Award; Bible translation of Book of Ruth in Naga Sign Language.
  • 2023: Training for personnel from Manipur and Sikkim; Bible translation of Book of Ephesians.
  • 2024: Collaboration with SCERT for Certificate Course on Sign Language; Employed 5 Deaf teachers.
  • 2025: All Class 10 students pass; Showcase at Youthnet Nagaland; Sign Language Ministry launch by Dimapur Ao Baptist Arogo.
  • 2026: Admissions open for academic year; Recent spiritual additions to the community.

Collaborations

DBM has an MoU with the Government of Nagaland and is a lifetime member of the India Council for Child Welfare. It was formerly a member of the Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) and collaborates with SCERT Nagaland for sign language training.

DBM provides interpretation services for police, courts, media, and government departments, and supports research in linguistics and disabilities.
